How to log in to Pilot Institute.

Common Issue

Many students search for "Pilot Institute Login" on Google and end up clicking on outdated or broken links, including old Teachable login pages that are no longer supported.

The correct way to log in:

  1. Open your browser and go directly to pilotinstitute.com.
  2. Click the "Login" button in the top-right corner of the homepage.
  3. Enter the email address and password you used when you purchased your course.
  4. If you are redirected to an old Teachable page, stop and return to pilotinstitute.com to log in from there instead.
⚠️ Important. Do not use Google search results to find the login page. Bookmark pilotinstitute.com directly to avoid outdated links.

How to access QuizBank for quizzes, final exams, and reviews.

Course Feature

QuizBank is where you can take practice quizzes, complete your final exams, and review previous attempts.

  1. Log in to your Pilot Institute account at pilotinstitute.com.
  2. Navigate to your enrolled course from the dashboard.
  3. Look for the "QuizBank" section in the course sidebar or menu.
  4. From here, you can start new quizzes, take final exams, or click on past attempts to review your answers.
💡 Tip. If QuizBank does not appear, make sure you have completed the required course sections that unlock it.

How to access flashcards.

Course Feature

Flashcards are a study tool available within your course. Please note that the older mobile app does not fully support flashcards on many newer and some older devices.

  1. Log in at pilotinstitute.com on a desktop or laptop computer for the best experience.
  2. Open your enrolled course from the dashboard.
  3. Look for the "Flashcards" section within the course content.
⚠️ Note about the mobile app. The current app has limited compatibility with many devices. For the most reliable flashcard experience, please use a web browser on a computer until the app is fully updated.

How to download files from DOCS within the course.

Course Feature

Course materials, study guides, and reference documents are available for download from the DOCS section.

  1. Log in and navigate to your course.
  2. In the course menu, find and click on the "DOCS" tab or section.
  3. Browse the available files and click on the one you would like to download.
  4. A download prompt should appear. If it does not, try right-clicking the file link and selecting "Save Link As...".
💡 Tip. If downloads are being blocked, check that your browser is not blocking pop-ups from pilotinstitute.com. You can adjust this in your browser settings.

What is Circle?

Community

Circle is the community platform used by Pilot Institute. It is where students can connect with each other, ask questions, participate in live events, and access additional learning resources.

Think of it as the social and support hub for all Pilot Institute students. Once you have access, you can join discussions, attend live Q&A sessions, and more.

💡 Good to know. Circle is separate from the main course platform. You may need to create a Circle account or log in separately the first time.

How to reset your password.

Common Issue

The "Forgot Password" feature on the site can be unreliable. Here is what to try, and what to do if it does not work.

Try the self-service reset first:

  1. Go to pilotinstitute.com and click "Login".
  2. Click the "Forgot Password?" link below the password field.
  3. Enter the email address associated with your account and submit.
  4. Check your inbox (and spam/junk folder) for a reset email.
  5. Follow the link in the email to create a new password.
⚠️ If the reset email does not arrive. This is a known issue that affects some accounts. Do not worry—simply reach out to our support team and we will manually reset your password for you. This is quick and hassle-free.

How to manually mark videos as complete.

Course Feature

Sometimes, videos you have already watched may not show as completed due to a progress tracking issue. You can manually mark them.

  1. Navigate to the specific lesson or video in your course.
  2. Look for a checkmark button, "Mark as Complete" option, or a similar toggle near the video player or lesson title.
  3. Click it to manually mark the lesson as viewed.
  4. Repeat for any other lessons that are not showing as complete.
💡 Tip. If there is no manual completion option or the button does not work, contact support and we can update your progress on our end.

How to collect screenshots and system information for troubleshooting.

Account / Tech

When you contact support about a technical issue, sending screenshots and system details helps us resolve it much faster.

How to take a screenshot:

  1. Windows: Press Windows Key + Shift + S to open the Snipping Tool. Select the area you want to capture, then paste it into an email or chat.
  2. Mac: Press Command + Shift + 4 to select an area and capture it. The screenshot will save to your desktop.
  3. Chromebook: Press Ctrl + Shift + Show Windows (the rectangle key on the top row).
  4. Phone/Tablet: Press the Power + Volume Down buttons at the same time (most devices).

How to find your system and browser information:

  1. Open your browser and go to whatismybrowser.com.
  2. The site will automatically detect your browser, operating system, and other useful details.
  3. Take a screenshot of this page or copy the information and include it when you contact support.
💡 Why this helps. Knowing your browser version, operating system, and screen resolution lets our team reproduce and fix your issue faster.

How your device, browser, and network should be set up.

Account / Tech

If you are experiencing issues loading the site, playing videos, or accessing course content, the problem may be related to your device, browser, or network settings.

Browser requirements:

  1. Use a modern, up-to-date browser such as Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Microsoft Edge, or Safari.
  2. Make sure your browser is updated to the latest version.
  3. Enable JavaScript and cookies in your browser settings.

Clear your cache and cookies:

  1. Go to your browser's Settings → Privacy (or History).
  2. Select "Clear Browsing Data" or "Clear History".
  3. Make sure Cached images/files and Cookies are checked, then clear.
  4. Restart your browser and try again.

Network considerations:

  1. If you are on a work or school network, the site may be blocked by a firewall. Try using a personal network or mobile hotspot.
  2. Disable any VPN or proxy services temporarily to see if they are causing the issue.
  3. Make sure your internet connection is stable. Try loading other websites to confirm.

How to access "Zero to Remote Pilot" to start or leave it.

Course Feature

"Zero to Remote Pilot" is a guided learning path designed to take you from beginner to certified. Here is how to access or exit it.

To start:

  1. Log in to your account at pilotinstitute.com.
  2. From your dashboard, look for the "Zero to Remote Pilot" option or link.
  3. Click to enroll or begin. Follow the guided steps from there.

To leave or exit:

  1. If you would like to exit the "Zero to Remote Pilot" track and access your courses individually, look for an option to "Leave" or "Exit" the program from within the track overview page.
  2. If you do not see this option, contact support and we will adjust your enrollment.

How to request an endorsement.

Account / Tech

Endorsement requests are built directly into the course. The option is mentioned at two points during the curriculum, but here is a quick walkthrough in case you need it.

  1. Complete all required sections of your course, including the final exam.
  2. Once you have met the requirements, an "Request Endorsement" button or link will appear within the course.
  3. Click it and follow the on-screen instructions to submit your request.
  4. Our team will review your request and process the endorsement.
💡 Tip. If you cannot find the endorsement request option after completing the course, reach out to support and we will walk you through it or process it for you.
